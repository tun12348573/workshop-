**FCAJ Community Day** was a community sharing event for students and learners interested in AWS, Cloud, AI, and software development. - Helping participants build a more effective learning method.

- Introducing an AI-assisted software development method with clear process, documents, and roles.
- Creating an environment for young learners to practice sharing, presenting, and learning from the community.

## 1. Session 1 - How to Become "Addicted to Learning" Like Social Media or Online Games

### Main Content

The first session focused on a topic that is very familiar to students and technology learners: **why people can easily become addicted to social media and online games, but find it difficult to maintain consistent learning habits.**

The speaker started with a common situation: many people tell themselves that they will only play games for 30 minutes or scroll social media for 5 minutes, but in the end, they spend a lot of time without studying anything. The reason is not necessarily laziness. Instead, the brain has not been placed in a learning environment that is attractive enough to create motivation.

### Why Are Social Media and Games So Attractive?

The speaker analyzed several characteristics that make social media and online games highly engaging.

#### Fast Rewards

When playing games, players can quickly receive a feeling of victory, such as defeating an opponent, completing a mission, or leveling up.

When scrolling social media, users can quickly encounter an interesting video, funny content, or information that matches their interests.

These fast rewards make the brain feel excited.

#### Curiosity

Users do not know what the next TikTok or social media video will be. This uncertainty stimulates curiosity and encourages people to keep scrolling.

This is similar to a loop:

```text
Curiosity
  ↓
Try
  ↓
Explore
  ↓
Satisfaction
  ↓
Want to try again
```

#### Low Effort

Scrolling social media or playing games requires almost no preparation. Users can simply sit or lie down and start immediately.

Studying, on the other hand, usually requires more steps, such as sitting at a desk, opening a laptop, preparing materials, focusing, and maintaining attention for a long period of time.

### Why Is Studying Difficult to Maintain?

Long pointed out that studying often has characteristics opposite to games and social media:

- Results come slowly.
- It requires long-term focus.
- It requires overcoming boredom at the beginning.
- There is no instant reward.
- It is easy to procrastinate when the brain feels tired or unmotivated.

For example, when playing a game, a person can know whether they win or lose after 30 minutes or 1 hour. However, learning a skill such as AWS, programming, or English may take months before the results become clear.

### Dopamine and the Reward Expectation Mechanism

An important point presented in this session was that dopamine is not only released when people receive a reward. It can also be released when people **expect that a reward is coming soon**.

For example:

- When playing a game, players know they are about to win or defeat an opponent.
- When scrolling TikTok, users expect that the next video may be interesting.
- In games of chance, people are attracted by the uncertainty of what the next reward will be.

From this, Long suggested that this mechanism can be applied to learning by creating small rewards and elements of surprise.

### How to Apply This to Learning

#### Create Random Rewards

One suggested method is to write many small rewards on sticky notes, fold them, and put them in a box.

After a short study session, for example 10 minutes, the learner can pick one note from the box.

Possible rewards include:

- Take a 5-minute break.
- Drink water.
- Eat a small snack.
- Listen to one song.
- "Good luck next time."

The important point is not to create rewards that are too large, such as playing games for 2 hours, because that may pull the learner away from studying.

#### Use the Fear of Losing a Streak

Long mentioned the streak mechanism used in TikTok, games, or Duolingo.

People usually fear losing something they already have. Therefore, if learners create a daily study streak, they will have more motivation to maintain it.

How to apply it:

- Use a calendar and mark every day that includes studying.
- Studying only 5 to 10 minutes per day is still acceptable.
- After 1 week, 2 weeks, or 1 month, give yourself a small reward.
- Ask friends or family to check your progress to increase commitment.

#### Break Goals Into Smaller Parts

A major problem in learning is feeling overwhelmed. For example, AWS has many services, so learners may not know where to start.

Long compared this with running. If a beginner looks at a 5 km route from the beginning, it may feel exhausting. But if the route is divided into shorter sections, running becomes easier.

Applying this to studying:

- Do not try to study 5 hours from the beginning.
- Start with 10 minutes.
- Learn one small part each day.
- Learn one AWS service or one small concept per day.
- Gradually increase the study duration when it becomes a habit.

#### The 2-Minute Rule

The 2-minute rule was mentioned as a way to fight procrastination.

If something can be completed within 2 minutes, do it immediately instead of delaying it.

Examples:

- Open the learning material.
- Open a code file.
- Read one page.
- Write one note.
- Create an AWS account.
- Open one lab exercise.

#### Create Feedback Like a Game

Games often have experience points, levels, ranks, and rewards. Learners can apply the same mechanism:

- Gain XP for studying each day.
- Level up after completing a lab.
- Reward yourself after solving 10 questions.
- Receive a small reward after studying continuously for one week.
- Join a community to share progress.

#### Turn Social Media Habits Into Learning Habits

Long suggested that instead of using social media to follow drama, learners can use it to follow knowledge:

- Follow new AWS services.
- Read technology blogs.
- Share newly learned knowledge.
- Rewrite lessons as blog posts.
- Be confident when presenting in the community.

### Lessons Learned From Session 1

- Learners are not necessarily lazy; they may simply not know how to create a suitable learning environment.
- The brain likes fast rewards, curiosity, and the feeling of progress.
- Game mechanisms such as streaks, XP, and small rewards can be applied to studying.
- Breaking goals into smaller parts helps reduce the feeling of being overwhelmed.
- The winner is not always the most hardworking person, but the person who builds an environment that makes them want to study every day.

## 2. Session 2 - Ultimate Prompt Engineering and the Proptimizer Project on AWS

### Main Content

The next session introduced **Prompt Engineering**, which is the technique of communicating effectively with AI to receive better results.

Thinh started with a common problem: many people use AI but receive answers that do not match their expectations, are incomplete, or do not fit the context. The usual reason is that the prompt is unclear, lacks information, or is too generic.

### Problems With Poor Prompts

Thinh emphasized that:

- A generic prompt will produce a generic output.
- A prompt without enough context may produce results that do not match the user's needs.
- Ambiguous prompts can cause inconsistent AI responses.
- Sending too many unnecessary tokens can increase AI usage costs.
- Poor communication with AI can reduce productivity instead of improving it.

### Components of a Good Prompt

A good prompt should include the following main components:

- **Role**: What role the AI should play.
- **Task / Instruction**: What the AI should do.
- **Context**: The background of the problem.
- **Input**: The input data.
- **Output Format**: The expected format of the result.
- **Examples**: Examples that help the AI understand better.
- **Constraints**: Rules or limitations that the AI should follow.

For example, instead of writing:

```text
Help me improve my CV.
```

A better prompt could be:

```text
You are a career coach for fresh graduates.
Please improve my internship CV.
Context: I am a final-year software engineering student applying for a frontend internship.
Output format: Provide bullet points and rewrite suggestions.
Constraints: Keep it professional and concise.
```

### Best Practices for Writing Prompts

Thinh shared several principles for using AI effectively:

- Be clear and specific.
- Use direct language.
- Provide as much relevant information as possible.
- Separate sections in the prompt using delimiters.
- Describe what you want the AI to do instead of only saying what you do not want.
- Allow the AI to answer "I don't know" when there is not enough information.
- Do not ask the AI to do too many tasks in one prompt.
- Break large tasks into smaller tasks to get better responses.

### Tokens and AI Usage Cost

Thinh also explained the concept of tokens.

A token is a unit used by AI models to process text. A sentence or a word can be split into multiple tokens. AI usage cost usually depends on:

- Input tokens.
- Output tokens.

One notable point is that token count may differ across languages. Vietnamese text can require more tokens than English, so processing the same amount of content may cost more when using Vietnamese.

### Advanced Techniques

The session also mentioned several advanced Prompt Engineering techniques.

#### Chain of Thought

The AI is guided to think step by step to solve complex problems.

#### Self-Consistency

The AI generates multiple reasoning paths and then selects the most suitable or consistent result.

#### Tree of Thought

The AI explores multiple branches of reasoning, similar to searching through a tree to find a better solution.

### Proptimizer Project Demo

After the theory section, Thinh demonstrated a personal project called **Proptimizer**.

Proptimizer is an application used to optimize prompts. Users enter an initial prompt, and the system rewrites it into a more complete prompt so that the user can receive better results from AI.

For example, a simple prompt:

```text
Help me learn React.
```

can be optimized into a more detailed prompt, where the AI is asked to act as a React expert and guide the learner with a clear and structured learning path.

### AWS Architecture of Proptimizer

Thinh presented the architecture of the Proptimizer project using several AWS services.

#### Amazon CloudFront

CloudFront is used as a CDN to deliver website content with low latency and improve global access performance.

#### Amazon S3

S3 is used to store the frontend, static website files, images, or other static assets.

Thinh also mentioned S3 as a flexible storage service that can store many types of data, such as images, CSV files, Word files, or raw data.

#### Amazon Cognito

Cognito is used to manage user authentication and authorization. It supports sign-up, sign-in, password reset, and JWT token generation.

The benefit is that developers do not need to build their own database to store usernames and passwords.

#### Amazon API Gateway

API Gateway manages requests between the frontend and backend. It supports API routing, rate limiting, and authorization integration.

#### AWS Lambda

Lambda is used as the serverless backend. Developers only need to upload code to Lambda, while AWS manages the server, scaling, and execution when events are triggered.

Thinh compared Lambda with EC2. If EC2 is used for backend, users must manage servers, VPCs, subnets, and many infrastructure configurations. With Lambda, server management is significantly reduced.

#### Amazon Bedrock

Amazon Bedrock is used to access foundation models from different providers. In the project, Bedrock supports prompt optimization without requiring the developer to train a model.

Bedrock can also be combined with guardrails to control input and output.

#### Amazon DynamoDB

DynamoDB is a NoSQL database used to store user data, prompts, and prompt optimization history. It provides millisecond response time and supports automatic scaling.

Thinh distinguished DynamoDB from S3: DynamoDB is a database, while S3 is storage.

#### Amazon CloudWatch

CloudWatch Logs is used to collect logs from Lambda and API Gateway, supporting debugging and performance analysis.

CloudWatch Metrics is used to track metrics such as API latency, system errors, and application performance.

### Demo Experience

In the demo, Thinh opened the Proptimizer application hosted through CloudFront. The application allowed users to enter a prompt, optimize it, and copy the optimized prompt to another AI tool to receive a better answer.

Besides the website, Thinh also introduced a browser extension. Users can select text on a website, click the extension icon, and optimize the prompt directly.

The extension also includes an AI chat feature, allowing users to ask AI questions from any website without leaving the browser.

### Lessons Learned From Session 2

- Prompt Engineering is an important skill when working with AI.
- Providing relevant context helps AI answer more accurately.
- A prompt should include role, task, context, input, output format, examples, and constraints.
- Tokens matter because they affect cost.
- AI can hallucinate when prompts are unclear or contexts are mixed.
- Techniques such as Chain of Thought, Self-Consistency, and Tree of Thought can improve response quality.
- AWS can be used to build a real AI application using CloudFront, S3, Cognito, API Gateway, Lambda, Bedrock, DynamoDB, and CloudWatch.

## 3. Session 3 - Career Mindset in the AI Era

### Speaker Introduction

The next session was shared by **Khang**, currently a **Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics**. He shared that he had also experienced confusion like many current students, not knowing his direction or what to prepare before working.

Instead of focusing on a specific technical topic, this session focused on a more practical question:

```text
Why are you not working yet?
```

### Context: AI Is Changing How People Learn and Work

Khang mentioned that there are currently many AI tools, chatbots, and new terms appearing continuously. This makes many learners feel overloaded.

Common questions include:

- Will AI replace freshers or interns?
- Will AI remove opportunities for junior developers?
- How can AI help me?
- Should I use AI during interviews?
- Is it acceptable to use AI for assignments?

### Foundation Is Still the Most Important Thing

One main message of the session was: in a world that changes very fast, learners need to focus on things that **do not change**.

Those things include:

- Foundation.
- Fundamental thinking.
- Basic knowledge of infrastructure.
- Core knowledge of software.
- If studying AI, understanding the foundation of AI.
- Thought process, which means the process of thinking and solving problems.

Companies do not expect freshers to know every framework, programming language, or all AWS services. What matters more is having a strong foundation and the ability to think correctly.

### AI Amplifies the User

A very important message was:

```text
AI amplifies you.
```

This means:

- If you are weak, AI may make you weaker because you do not understand what it generates.
- If you are good, AI will help you become better and increase your productivity.
- AI can support thinking, brainstorming, and developing ideas.
- However, AI cannot replace your real understanding.

Khang emphasized that learners can "outsource thinking" at the brainstorming level with AI, but they cannot outsource their understanding. AI can provide results, but if users do not understand those results, they will not be able to solve the problem when the requirement changes slightly.

### How Recruiters Evaluate Assignments

Khang shared that when evaluating assignments, correctness is not always the most important thing. Recruiters often care more about:

- How the candidate thinks.
- Why the candidate chooses a certain solution.
- Why one service is chosen instead of another.
- Whether the candidate understands trade-offs.
- Whether the candidate can explain the architecture.
- Whether the candidate understands the code or architecture generated by AI.

For example, if a candidate chooses Lambda instead of EC2, the important point is being able to explain why:

- Why is Lambda suitable?
- Why not EC2?
- Does the workload need serverless?
- Are there any limitations?
- What are the trade-offs?

### The Question "Why" Is Very Important

One keyword emphasized in the session was:

```text
WHY
```

Learners should continuously ask:

- Why did I choose this solution?
- Is there a better way?
- Why did I use this service?
- If the assumption changes, is the solution still valid?
- If the system becomes larger, is the architecture still suitable?

Khang advised learners to practice asking "why" multiple times to reach the root cause.

### Embrace Mistakes

Another important point was that beginners should be allowed to make mistakes, as long as they learn from them.

Khang explained that when companies hire freshers or interns, they also need to account for the cost of mistakes. This is normal because mistakes are part of the learning process.

However, learners need to:

- Dare to do.
- Dare to make mistakes.
- Know how to fix mistakes.
- Learn from mistakes.
- Not avoid responsibility.

### Integrity

Khang emphasized **integrity**, which can be understood as honesty, responsibility, and doing the right thing even when no one is checking.

In real work, there are many things that managers or team leads cannot check in detail. However, workers still need to do them properly and completely.

Examples:

- Cleaning up resources after finishing work.
- Deleting test resources after using a shared account.
- Handling edge cases in functions.
- Not only implementing the visible requirements, but also thinking about hidden cases.
- Avoiding leaving consequences for other people.

Khang connected this with long-term thinking. The value of integrity may not be visible immediately, but in the long run, it creates trust within a team and society.

### Long-Term Thinking When Choosing a Job

Khang shared that when choosing a job, people should not only look at salary. A job should be evaluated from multiple perspectives.

Benefits may include:

- Salary.
- Experience.
- Network.
- Knowledge.
- Portfolio or long-term development opportunities.

Salary is important, but it is not the only factor. A job with a lower salary but better learning opportunities, good mentors, real projects, and a strong network may have greater long-term value.

### Three Circles When Choosing a Job

Khang described that a job can include three parts:

- What you love to do.
- What you have to do even if you do not like it.
- The benefits you receive.

Not every job has all three parts perfectly. However, if a job provides an opportunity where these elements overlap, it may be worth considering.

### Criteria for Evaluating Freshers

For freshers, recruiters usually do not expect too much real work experience. Some factors mentioned include:

- Attitude.
- Education and qualifications.
- Relevant skills.
- Experience.
- Exposure.
- Personal qualities.

Among these, attitude is very important. If a candidate does not have much experience yet, they need to show a learning mindset, responsibility, and growth potential.

### Suggested Next Actions

Khang suggested three actions.

#### Ask More Questions

Learners should be brave enough to ask questions. The more questions they ask, the better they become at asking the right questions.

If no one answers, they should make assumptions and continue moving forward instead of stopping.

#### Think Long Term

Learners should not choose opportunities only for short-term benefits. They should consider experience, network, knowledge, and future development opportunities.

#### Do Not Go Alone

Building a project alone is difficult. If learners work on a project as a team, they can show not only technical skills but also:

- Teamwork.
- Work management.
- Communication.
- Task division.
- Supporting teammates.
- Leadership or collaboration potential.

### Lessons Learned From Session 3

- AI does not fully replace learners, but it amplifies the user's ability.
- Foundation remains the core factor.
- AI cannot replace real understanding.
- Recruiters evaluate thought process more than final results.
- Learners need to keep asking "why."
- Making mistakes and learning from them is part of growing.
- Integrity is important in long-term work.
- When choosing a job, do not only look at salary; also consider experience, network, knowledge, and the future.
- Building projects in a team helps demonstrate more skills.

## 4. Session 4 - BMAD Method and Process-Based AI-Assisted Software Development

### Speaker Introduction

The final session in the transcript was presented by **Thao**, currently a **Software Developer at Vietnam International Bank (VIB)**.

The main topic was AI-assisted software development, especially the issue of many people using AI to code while skipping the software development process, which results in code that is difficult to control.

### The Problem With Vibe Coding

Thao started by discussing "vibe coding" - a way many people use AI to code by continuously prompting AI to create an application.

The common expectation is:

- Give an idea to AI.
- Prompt continuously.
- AI understands all requirements.
- AI creates a complete application.

However, the actual results often include:

- AI gradually forgets old context.
- The longer the prompting continues, the more confusing the system becomes.
- Generated code lacks structure.
- New features break old features.
- The project becomes messy and difficult to maintain.
- The application cannot be deployed as a real product.

The core problem is that users skip the **Software Development Lifecycle** and hand the entire process of design, planning, analysis, and development to AI without a clear structure.

### What Is the BMAD Method?

Thao introduced a method called the **BMAD Method**.

BMAD Method is a method that supports AI-assisted software development with process, roles, documents, and control. Instead of prompting freely and letting AI do everything, BMAD Method divides the development process into clear stages.

According to the presentation, BMAD Method has the following characteristics:

- Open source.
- Free to use.
- Structured according to the software development process.
- Defines multiple roles in a development team.
- Supports planning, analysis, architecture, development, and review.
- Can be integrated with IDEs or AI coding tools.
- Helps AI better understand the context and expected output.

### Comparing Vibe Coding and BMAD Method

#### Vibe Coding

- Prompting AI directly.
- No clear design.
- No complete documentation.
- Context becomes longer and easier to lose.
- Updating or changing features can easily cause chain errors.
- AI may hallucinate or misunderstand requirements.
- Code becomes difficult to control when the project grows.

#### BMAD Method

- Breaks the process down according to software development standards.
- Includes documents such as PRD, architecture, epic, and story.
- Defines clear roles such as PM, Architect, PO, Scrum Master, Developer, and QA.
- Each step is handled separately.
- Context becomes smaller and clearer.
- AI understands requirements more easily.
- Generated code is more controlled and easier to review.

### Roles in BMAD Method

Thao explained that BMAD Method defines many roles similar to a real software development team.

Some roles mentioned include:

- Product Manager.
- Architect.
- Product Owner.
- Scrum Master.
- Developer.
- QA / Reviewer.

When using BMAD Method, users can call the correct role for the task. For example, when product requirements need to be created, the Product Manager role can be used. When system architecture needs to be designed, the Architect Agent can be used.

This helps AI understand what role it is playing and what output it needs to produce.

### Development Process With BMAD Method

#### Phase 1 - Planning

In the first stage, the user gives the initial idea to AI.

Example:

```text
I want to build a task management application.
```

Then AI brainstorms with the user to clarify:

- Who the application is for.
- What the main features are.
- What output is expected.
- What the user flow looks like.
- What requirements need attention.

From there, the Product Manager Agent can create a PRD or product requirement document.

#### Phase 2 - Architecture

After the PRD is created, the Architect Agent analyzes the document and proposes a technical architecture.

The output of this phase may include:

- System architecture.
- Technology stack.
- Database design.
- API design.
- Integration design.
- Technical constraints.

The goal is to transform product requirements into a clear technical design.

#### Phase 3 - Sharding

After the large documents are created, BMAD Method splits them into smaller parts.

The concepts mentioned include:

- Epic.
- Story.
- Subtask.

For example, a large feature such as Authentication can be divided into smaller stories:

- Login UI.
- Register UI.
- Login API.
- Register API.
- JWT token.
- Route protection middleware.
- Input validation.
- Error handling.

This division helps AI work with a shorter and clearer context.

#### Phase 4 - Development

When the stories are clearly defined, the Developer Agent starts coding each story separately.

The process may work as follows:

1. Find a story with the approved status.
2. Split the story into smaller subtasks.
3. Code according to the requirements of the story.
4. Update the story status to review.
5. QA or Review Agent checks the result.
6. If there are errors, return to fixing.
7. Repeat until the requirement is satisfied.

The important point is that AI should not do everything at once. Humans still control which story is approved, which story is blocked, and which story should be implemented.

### Story Status Management

The session also mentioned that stories can have different statuses, such as:

- Draft.
- Approved.
- Blocked.
- Review.
- Done.

Humans are responsible for controlling these statuses. This helps prevent AI from coding too much by itself, going in the wrong direction, or overlapping features.

### Reducing AI Hallucination

One major benefit of BMAD Method is reducing hallucination.

When users continuously prompt AI in a long conversation, the context window becomes very large and AI can mix up information. But when the project is divided into roles, documents, epics, and stories, AI only needs to focus on the small part currently being processed.

This helps:

- AI understand the context better.
- Reduce overlapping knowledge.
- Reduce errors caused by forgetting old requirements.
- Limit the impact between different features.
- Make review and bug fixing easier.

### Team Mode Capability

BMAD Method also supports working with multiple roles in the same project.

For example, the Architect and Developer may have different perspectives. In that case, multiple roles can join the discussion, challenge each other, and make better decisions.

This simulates how a real software development team works, instead of having only one AI agent do everything.

### Philosophy of BMAD Method

An important idea in this session is that the method is not only about how the software code is written, but also about **how the document is written**.

If the document is clear, the requirements are clear, and the architecture is clear, AI can generate better code.

In other words:

```text
Good document → AI understands better → Better code
```

### Lessons Learned From Session 4

- Do not hand the entire project to AI using only one long prompt.
- Vibe coding can be useful for small demos, but it can easily create messy code in large projects.
- AI-assisted software development still needs the Software Development Lifecycle.
- PRD, architecture, epic, story, and review are still necessary.
- Breaking tasks into smaller parts helps reduce AI hallucination.
- Humans still need to control requirements, status, and quality.
- AI should support the development team, not replace the entire process.
- The clearer the document, the better the AI output.
